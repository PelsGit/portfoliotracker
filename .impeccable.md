## Design Context

### Users
Primary user is the developer/owner — personal portfolio tracking tool used daily for a quick at-a-glance read on portfolio performance. Future goal: make it available online to other investors. Typical context: desktop, sitting at a desk, checking in the morning or after market close.

### Brand Personality
Calm, precise, direct. The interface should feel like a well-designed financial instrument — not a consumer app trying to be fun, not a Bloomberg terminal trying to be intimidating. Think: confident restraint. Every number should be instantly readable, every piece of data immediately clear.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Inspiration**: Getquin — clean dark UI, dense-but-airy information layout, numbers that breathe
- **Theme**: Dark only. Background deep navy/charcoal, not pure black.
- **Colors**: Accents must pop against the dark background. Current `#6c8cff` blue and `#34d399` green / `#f87171` red exist but feel muted. Colors need higher perceived luminance on dark surfaces — shift toward OKLCH for perceptual accuracy.
- **Anti-reference**: Avoid the AI dashboard cliché — no glowing neon cyan, no purple-to-blue gradients, no glassmorphism, no hero metric layout with giant numbers and gradient accent bars.
- **Typography**: No custom font yet (system-ui fallback). Could benefit from a distinctive but readable typeface — something precise, slightly technical but not monospace-as-a-cliché.

### Design Principles
1. **Information density without clutter** — show everything needed at a glance; no extra clicks for key metrics
2. **Numbers first** — financial data is the product; typography and color serve legibility of numbers above all else
3. **Perceptual accuracy** — positive/negative states must be instantly distinguishable; use OKLCH for color consistency on dark backgrounds
4. **Calm confidence** — no motion for its own sake, no decoration that competes with data
5. **Dark-native** — don't treat dark as an afterthought; every color choice should be made for dark surfaces specifically
